The Beekman

The Beekman, a Thompson Hotel is a historic luxury hotel located in the Financial District of Lower Manhattan, known for its stunning architecture and rich heritage. Originally built in the 19th century, the building features a magnificent nine-story atrium topped with a pyramidal glass skylight, which is one of its most iconic elements. The hotel beautifully blends vintage charm with modern sophistication, offering elegantly designed rooms with a mix of classic and contemporary décor. Each room is equipped with high-end amenities, ensuring comfort and luxury for guests. One of the highlights of The Beekman is its renowned dining options, including the famous Temple Court restaurant, which serves exceptional cuisine in a stylish setting. The hotel also features a vibrant bar that attracts both locals and tourists. Its central location provides easy access to major attractions such as the Brooklyn Bridge, Wall Street, and One World Trade Center. Guests can explore the rich history and culture of New York City while staying in a peaceful and refined environment. The Beekman is known for its warm hospitality and personalized service, making every stay memorable. Its unique architectural design and historic significance set it apart from other hotels in the city. The ambiance of the hotel reflects a timeless elegance, with intricate iron railings and classic detailing throughout the property. It is a popular choice for travelers who appreciate history, luxury, and distinctive design. Overall, The Beekman offers a perfect combination of heritage, style, and modern comfort, making it one of the most unique and desirable hotels in New York City.
